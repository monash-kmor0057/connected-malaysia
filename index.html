Section 1

Malaysia became connected

From fewer than 20,000 broadband subscribers in 2002 to over 30 million by 2015, Malaysia's digital leap was rapid — driven first by fixed infrastructure, then by an explosion in mobile connectivity.

Section 2

How Malaysians use the internet

Communication and entertainment dominate online life. WhatsApp connects nearly every Malaysian, while YouTube leads video streaming. Mobile gaming has surged — but device preferences differ sharply between fixed and mobile broadband users.

Section 3

Faster internet ≠ perfect experience

Consumer satisfaction improved across all activities from 2023 to 2024. Online gaming showed the biggest leap. But web browsing, voice calls, and video calls still fall short of what users expect — the gap between expectation and reality persists.

Section 4

The speed divide: progress across Malaysia

Every state gained speed between 2020 and 2026 — but the gains are uneven. Johor and Selangor surged ahead, while East Malaysia and northern states still trail. Speed and experience improvements do not automatically mean equal digital access.

Key Takeaways

What the data tells us

📈
Malaysia's connectivity has grown dramatically over the past two decades — from near-zero broadband to 30+ million subscribers.
💬
Internet is essential in daily life, led by communication and entertainment — WhatsApp and YouTube dominate.
⭐
Experience quality has improved, but gaming and some services — especially voice and video calls — still need attention.
🗺️
Speed improvements are uneven — Johor and Selangor gained the most; East Malaysia and northern states still lag behind.
⚡
Better speed does not automatically mean equal digital experience. Rural-urban and inter-state gaps persist.
